# Synthetic Demo Data Legal, Privacy, and Ethical Governance Framework

## Purpose

This document defines the legal, privacy, ethical, and responsible-use governance layer for the synthetic Microsoft 365 E5 / Microsoft Purview banking and financial-services simulation platform.

It explains how synthetic demo data, fictional personas, generated telemetry, AI-generated content, simulated incidents, and executive storytelling should be governed responsibly.

This framework is intended for demo builders, presenters, consultants, Codex agents, workshop facilitators, security architects, AI governance advisors, and managed-service teams.

---

## Core Responsible-Use Thesis

> Synthetic data reduces risk only when it is clearly fictional, responsibly generated, accurately represented, and never mixed with real customer, employee, legal, HR, financial, or incident data.

The platform must preserve:

```text
synthetic-only boundaries
customer-demo separation
privacy-aware storytelling
anti-deception safeguards
safe AI content generation
neutral investigation language
clear disclaimers
responsible commercial positioning
```

---

## What This Platform Is

This platform is:

- a synthetic Microsoft 365 governance demo environment
- a cyber-range-style simulation framework
- a data-security and AI-governance storytelling system
- a Power BI / ADX / Purview / Sentinel conceptual lab
- a consulting and workshop acceleration asset
- a safe way to demonstrate realistic patterns without exposing real data

---

## What This Platform Is Not

This platform is not:

- production monitoring evidence
- legal advice
- regulatory certification
- HR disciplinary evidence
- proof of malicious user intent
- a production insider-risk investigation system
- a substitute for customer legal, privacy, HR, or compliance review
- a tool for profiling real employees
- a tool for generating real financial, HR, legal, or customer records

- real employee data
- real HR records
- real legal documents

- real government IDs
- real credentials
- real secrets
- real incident evidence
- real production telemetry

- real customer tenant URLs if sensitive
- real customer contracts or regulatory matters

---

## Customer-Demo Separation

When demonstrating to customers:

1. Do not load customer data into the synthetic lab.
2. Do not rename fictional personas to match customer employees.
3. Do not use real customer domains unless explicitly approved and safe.
4. Do not imply synthetic findings represent the customer's production environment.
5. Do not present synthetic incidents as real risk evidence.
6. Keep customer advisory findings separate from synthetic demo artifacts.

Recommended phrase:

```text
The patterns are realistic, but the users, files, identifiers, and telemetry are fictional.
```

---

## Presenter Obligations

Presenters must:

- disclose that the demo is synthetic
- avoid implying real user misconduct
- avoid overclaiming feature availability
- distinguish demo patterns from production evidence
- use neutral investigation language
- avoid exposing customer-sensitive information during Q&A
- validate current Microsoft licensing and feature availability before making implementation claims

---

## Anti-Deception Guidance

The platform should be realistic but not deceptive.

Do:

```text
state that data is synthetic
use fictional domains and identifiers
label screenshots or reports as synthetic when appropriate
explain that scenarios demonstrate patterns, not production findings
```

Do not:

```text
present synthetic incidents as real incidents
simulate real customer employees without permission
use real company names as fictional customers
create fake evidence intended to mislead
remove synthetic disclaimers from customer-facing assets
```

---

## AI-Generated Synthetic Content Rules

AI-generated content must:

- use fictional identities and entities
- avoid real secrets or credentials
- avoid real customer references
- avoid real financial account patterns without fictional prefixes
- avoid real HR or legal matter details
- include synthetic-only metadata when used in datasets
- be validated by safety checks before use in demos

AI-generated prompts and responses should be clearly designed for governance simulation, not real decision-making.

---

## Privacy Safeguards

The platform should avoid unnecessary personal-level framing even for synthetic users.

Recommended safeguards:

- use fictional personas consistently
- avoid unnecessary sensitive personal details
- avoid stigmatizing language
- preserve ambiguity in insider-risk-style scenarios
- separate severity from intent
- escalate HR/legal/privacy themes only as process examples

Preferred language:

```text
requires review
observed sequence
business context pending
potential exposure path
policy decision point
coaching opportunity
```

Avoid:

```text
guilty
malicious insider
data thief
confirmed exfiltration
employee misconduct proven
```

---

## Legal and Regulatory Positioning

This platform may support conversations about compliance, privacy, risk, and governance, but it does not provide legal conclusions.

Recommended disclaimer:

```text
This material is for educational, advisory, and synthetic demo purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice, regulatory certification, or production risk assessment.
```

When discussing regulated industries:

- keep recommendations conceptual unless validated by customer counsel
- avoid claiming compliance certification
- avoid mapping synthetic patterns directly to legal violations
- recommend formal legal/compliance review for production decisions

---

## Employee and Workforce Safeguards

Even synthetic insider-risk-style scenarios should be handled carefully.

Rules:

1. Do not equate synthetic risk chains with proof of intent.
2. Do not use the demo to normalize employee surveillance.
3. Always emphasize governance, privacy, HR, and Legal guardrails.
4. Include coaching and remediation pathways.
5. Avoid punitive framing by default.

Recommended phrase:

```text
Telemetry can show what happened, but it cannot fully explain intent. Context and governance process matter.
```

---

## Safe Storytelling Rules

Storytelling should clarify, not sensationalize.

Use:

```text
sequence creates meaning
AI inherits access
DLP is coaching plus control
the safe path must be easier than the risky path
signals require context
```

Avoid:

```text
fear-based claims
unsupported breach language
naming and shaming
claiming all AI usage is dangerous
claiming all DLP overrides are malicious
```

---

## Responsible Telemetry Generation

Generated telemetry must:

- be synthetic
- be reproducible when seeded
- use approved fictional patterns
- include scenario metadata
- include `IsSynthetic = true`
- avoid production identifiers
- avoid real domains unless safe and approved
- pass validation before demo use

Validation should check:

```text
real-looking secrets
real-looking credentials
non-fictional domains
missing IsSynthetic
invalid scenario IDs
unsafe identifier patterns
raw data placed in external zones unintentionally
```

---

## Responsible Use of Screenshots and Reports

Screenshots and reports should:

- include synthetic context where appropriate
- avoid customer-identifying overlays unless approved
- not be reused as production evidence
- not show real tenant names if not intended
- be reviewed before external publication

For public sharing:

```text
remove internal customer context
confirm all visible identifiers are fictional
include synthetic/demo language when needed
```
